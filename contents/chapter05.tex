\subsection{Observations}

Based on the results presented in Table \ref{tab:thumb_model_comparison} and the ROC curves in Figure \ref{fig:thumb_roc_comparison}(a) and (b), in the 2020 dataset, tree-based models such as Random Forest and XGBoost demonstrated superior discriminative power, both achieving an AUROC of 0.77. However, evaluated on the 2025 dataset, Logistic Regression proved more resilient, securing the highest accuracy (0.77) and F1-score (0.56). This shift indicates that simpler linear models may provide better cross-dataset stability, whereas complex tree models suffer from significant performance degradation.

Furthermore, Table \ref{tab:thumb_cross_dataset} and Figure \ref{fig:thumb_roc_comparison}(c) illustrate the results of explicit cross-cohort evaluation (trained on 2020, tested on 2025). While Random Forest achieved the highest accuracy (0.80), its discriminative balance remained limited (F1-score of 0.52). XGBoost provided the highest AUROC (0.71), suggesting it yields better instance ranking despite lower absolute recall. In contrast to internal validation, Logistic Regression struggled in this direct transfer scenario, indicating that linear assumptions may be insufficient for generalizing complex fingertip patterns across distinct temporal cohorts.

These findings suggest that tree-based feature selection improves transferability, although the trade-off between discrimination and calibration remains method-dependent.
